You are no spring chicken Making reference to someone that is not young with the inference that this is not a good thing or that the person may be limited in some way due to age. According to The Idioms online dictionary, the saying originated around 1700 in a British periodical called The Spectator. The author, Joseph Addison, said: “You ought to consider you are now past a chicken; this Humour, which was well enough in a Girl, is insufferable in one of your Motherly Character.” Source: no spring chicken meaning origin example sentence history (theidioms.com) AI: artist-Wyeth, Midjourney An_old_man_holding_an_old_chicken_next_to_a_young_woman_Wyeth style
