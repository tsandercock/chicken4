Feather your nest To add money to benefit yourself whether it be for retirement or by taking advantage of another. Origin: Said to have started in mid-1500. The saying reflected the observation that birds build warm, safe, and strong nests by feathering them. Theidioms.com also mentioned that the sayings is used to “refer to a situation when someone is taking advantage of others.” According to the freedictionary.com, the sayingIt appeared in the 1553 play Respublica, and was was a cliché by the eighteenth century.
