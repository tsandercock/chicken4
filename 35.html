Chicken Hearted A person who is not brave in the face of danger. Origin: Most often referred to a person used to refer to a person who is easily scared or cowardly. It is an indirect insult and doesn’t reflect one’s positive attributes. According to idiom.com, the saying was famous in the late 17th century. It has the same meaning as “faint- hearted” or “faint of heart,” meaning someone who is generally weak or afraid. Source: chicken hearted meaning origin example sentence history (theidioms.com) AI
